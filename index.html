Who it is for

Anyone who wants to learn, improve, and enjoy beach volleyball.

Whether you are discovering beach volleyball for the first time, looking for a better workout than you are getting in your social volleyball league, transitioning from indoor, or helping an athlete develop during the summer, you will find structured training, a welcoming community, and a clear path to improving your game.

Discover Beach Volleyball For adults who are new to the game.

Learn the fundamentals of beach volleyball in a welcoming environment designed for players who want instruction, exercise, and a fun way to meet people.

  • No previous experience required
  • Learn serving, passing, setting, attacking, and movement
  • Build confidence before joining leagues or open play
Beyond Social Leagues For active adults, social league players, and former athletes who want more touches, better workouts, and a clearer path to improvement.

Whether you are currently playing in social leagues with your friends/co-workers, transitioning from another sport, or looking for a more intentional training environment, these sessions help adults improve by connecting them with a community of motivated players.

  • More touches than traditional 6v6 leagues
  • Beach-specific skills and doubles strategy
  • Structured coaching in a supportive community
  • Opportunities to connect with other active adults
Indoor Volleyball Players For indoor athletes who are ready to transition their game to the sand.

Learn how beach volleyball changes movement patterns, decision-making, shot selection, ball control, communication, and conditioning.

Junior Athletes Structured summer development environment.

School Coaches For coaches looking to recommend meaningful summer training.

What you will improve

Skill development across the full beach game.

Technical Skills

Serving, passing, setting, attacking, defending, and blocking with cleaner mechanics and more confidence in the sand.

Tactical Skills + Game IQ

Shot selection, serving strategy, court positioning, reading opponents, and learning how to win points with intention.

Movement + Athleticism

Beach footwork, balance, approach patterns, defensive movement, conditioning, and efficient movement in deep sand.

Mental Game

Confidence, composure, competitiveness, resilience after mistakes, and focus during long rallies and close matches.

Partner + Team Dynamics

Communication, serve-receive responsibilities, defensive systems, trust, encouragement, and problem-solving during live play.

Coach Jara

Built from the path many newer beach players know well.

After moving to Chicago, I was introduced to the beach volleyball scene and quickly realized how difficult it can be for motivated adult players to find structured training, consistent feedback, and a serious path for improvement.

I came into beach volleyball with athleticism and indoor experience, but no formal beach background. That experience shaped how I coach today: technical detail, simple explanations, competitive standards, partner communication, and positive, high-energy training.

FAQ

Quick answers before you reach out.

Where are sessions held?

Sessions are primarily planned around Chicago lakefront sand courts, with select suburban sand courts possible depending on scheduling and group needs.

Do I need beach volleyball experience?

No. Beginners, recreational players, and indoor athletes transitioning to beach can all use the intake form to find the right starting point.

Can individuals join small group lessons?

Yes. Individuals can join group lessons when there is a good skill-level fit, and groups of friends or players can also book together.

Are juniors accepted?

Yes. Junior training is planned for ages 10-18, with a focus on fundamentals, beach transition skills, competitive preparation, and conditioning.

What level are adult camps for?

Adult camp tracks are planned for recreational players and B/BB intermediate players, with drop-in options possible in the future.

Is pricing listed?

Pricing varies by format. Rates can be shared after an intro call or intake form so the recommended option matches the player or group.

What should players bring?

Bring water, sun protection, athletic clothing, and any personal gear you prefer for training in the sand.
