What you will improve

Skill development across the full beach game.

Technical Skills

Serving, passing, setting, attacking, defending, and blocking with cleaner mechanics and more confidence in the sand.

Tactical Skills + Game IQ

Shot selection, serving strategy, court positioning, reading opponents, and learning how to win points with intention.

Movement + Athleticism

Beach footwork, balance, approach patterns, defensive movement, conditioning, and efficient movement in deep sand.

Mental Game

Confidence, composure, competitiveness, resilience after mistakes, and focus during long rallies and close matches.

Partner + Team Dynamics

Communication, serve-receive responsibilities, defensive systems, trust, encouragement, and problem-solving during live play.

What level am I?

Use this guide to choose the best starting point.

These levels help players self-assess before training. Court placement can still shift after coaches observe players so each group gets the right challenge, pace, and learning environment.

Level 1

Beginner (Recreational)

Brand new to the sport and still learning the rules, footwork, and basic court awareness.

  • Underhand or basic overhand serving
  • Learning platform control, footwork, and basic passing
  • Bump sets only, with no hand setting yet
  • Learning positions, calls, rotation, and court responsibilities
Level 2

B Level (Intermediate)

Plays rec ball or local leagues and is starting to get reps, improve skills, and take the game seriously.

  • Overhand serving with some placement
  • Occasional solid passes, but control and consistency are still developing
  • Developing bump setting and attempting hand sets on clean passes
  • Learning defensive angles, blocking timing, and in-game decisions
Level 3

BB Level (Upper Intermediate)

Low-to-mid competitive player with technical knowledge, tournament experience, and inconsistent execution.

  • Zone-targeted overhand or float serves
  • Can pass to the setter, but not consistently to the same target
  • Fairly consistent bump setting with some hand-set attempts
  • Communicates, targets opponents, and adapts strategy slowly
Level 4

A Level (Competitive)

Advanced club-level competitor with strong skills that may fluctuate under pressure or tough conditions.

  • Aggressive float or topspin serving with purpose
  • Solid free-ball passing, but inconsistent against strong servers
  • Confident bump and hand setting on good passes
  • Adapts game plans and runs some offensive options
Level 5

AA (Semi-Professional)

High-level competitive or semi-pro player with the tools and awareness to play consistently at a high level.

  • Consistent jump serve or hybrid serves that challenge opponents
  • 8-9/10 free balls passed perfectly and 5-6/10 serve receive passes in-system
  • Clean bump and hand setting under pressure
  • Thinks like a coach and adjusts match strategy mid-set
Level 6

Open (Professional)

Elite-level player competing on top circuits and training year-round with purpose, precision, and data.

  • Pressure-driven jump serves, float-short serves, and hybrid variations
  • 10/10 free balls passed perfectly and 7-8/10 serve receive passes in-system
  • Can set in rhythm from almost any location or pass
  • Executes advanced systems and uses 3-block / 4-block strategically
Do not stress about picking the perfect level.

Start with your best self-assessment. Coaches may move players between courts after observing play so each group supports everyone's learning. When in doubt, start one level lower to build confidence and solid fundamentals.

FAQ

Quick answers before you reach out.

Where are sessions held?

Sessions are primarily planned around Chicago lakefront sand courts, with select suburban sand courts possible depending on scheduling and group needs.

Do I need beach volleyball experience?

No. Beginners, recreational players, and indoor athletes transitioning to beach can all use the intake form to find the right starting point.

Can individuals join small group lessons?

Yes. Individuals can join group lessons when there is a good skill-level fit, and groups of friends or players can also book together.

Are juniors accepted?

Yes. Junior training is planned for ages 10-18, with a focus on fundamentals, beach transition skills, competitive preparation, and conditioning.

What level are adult camps for?

Adult camp tracks are planned for recreational players and B/BB intermediate players, with drop-in options possible in the future.

Is pricing listed?

Pricing varies by format. Rates can be shared after an intro call or intake form so the recommended option matches the player or group.

What should players bring?

Bring water, sun protection, athletic clothing, and any personal gear you prefer for training in the sand.
